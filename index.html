01 “Show, don’t tell” is the basic principle of modern storytelling, which I learned during my Degree in Audiovisual Communication at the University of Malaga and my Master's in Cinematography and Visual Arts at SchoolTraining.

Following this principle, my creativity kicked in to find a way to blend several fundamental concepts that my website needed to encompass: my personal and professional information, and the interaction of the website with the user in a fun and engaging manner.

02 Thanks to my background in cinematography, I knew I wanted to explore the quirky and absurd situations that the Rubber Hose Style animation offers.

In the design part, I focused on finding visual references of this style that would complement a design oriented towards proper UX/UI writing, using Figma, which I learned in the UX/UI Design Specialist course at Gauss.

03 Lastly, there was the development of the website. Thanks to my studies as a Full Stack Development Specialist at Deusto Formación and what I learned in the 42 Telefónica course in Malaga, I knew that one of the best ways to create that interaction with the user was through creating a canvas, using JavaScript code development and the jQuery library.

In addition to developing various codes to structure and organize the content of the page for all dispositives using HTML5, implementing visual styles with CSS, and creating images and videos with Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Premiere, After Effects and Midjourney's AI.

And this is the result! A page to introduce myself to the world.
